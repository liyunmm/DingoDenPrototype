Volunteer Experiences

Learn more about Dingo Den and catch a glimpse at what volunteering with us is like through the eyes of our very own volunteers!

Overall, my experience as a volunteer for the charity was a rewarding one. It was not only beneficial for my personal development in terms of honing in on my communication and people skills, but it was also a fantastic opportunity for me to expand my connections with different people and make new friends. An added bonus in this experience was getting to play a small yet significant role in a charity that focuses on an area that is not often discussed in society and which I am highly passionate about. - Annie, Volunteering since 2016

My volunteering experience for the charity was an overall positive and educating one. I believe it was rewarding largely in part because I got to play a role in conserving the fauna of Australia’s unique ecosystem by raising awareness to the Australian public about the state of the dingoes species. - Alex, Volunteering since 2018

Facts and Stats

Plus some extra facts for you to consider.

The Dingo has now been listed as ʻvunerableʼ by the IUCN.

Dingoes come in a variety of coat colours depending on geographical location.

Dingoes can run 60km/hour, travel 40km a day jump two metres high and successfully climb trees.

Dingoes do not bark like a dog but howl, chortle, yelp, whine, growl, chatter, snort, cough and purr.
